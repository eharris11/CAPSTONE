Learn About Scams

Understand common scam tactics and how to protect yourself

Phishing

Fake emails or messages pretending to be trusted companies.

Example: “Your bank account is locked. Click here to verify.”

Why it works: It looks official and urgent.

Tip: Never click links in unexpected emails.

Learn more

Urgency Tactics

Messages that pressure you to act immediately.

Example: “Act now or your account will be deleted.”

Why it works: Panic reduces critical thinking.

Tip: Slow down and verify first.

Learn more

Suspicious Links

Links that redirect to fake websites.

Example: “amazon-login-secure.net”

Why it works: Looks similar to real domains.

Tip: Always inspect URLs carefully.

Learn more

Impersonation Scams

Scammers pretend to be someone you trust.

Example: “Hi, it’s your boss—buy gift cards for me.”

Why it works: Uses authority and familiarity.

Tip: Always verify through another method.

Learn more

Lottery / Prize Scams

You “win” something you never entered.

Example: “You’ve won $10,000! Pay a fee to claim.”

Why it works: Exploits excitement and greed.

Tip: Legit prizes never require payment.

Learn more

Romance Scams

Fake relationships used to gain trust and money.

Example: “I need money for an emergency.”

Why it works: Emotional manipulation.

Tip: Never send money to someone you haven’t met.
