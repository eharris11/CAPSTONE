Module 1

Phishing

Fake emails or messages pretending to be trusted companies to steal your credentials.

Example: "Your bank account is locked. Click here to verify your identity."

Why it works: It looks official and creates urgency, making you react before thinking.

Protect yourself: Never click links in unexpected emails. Go directly to the official site.

Module 2

Urgency Tactics

Pressure messages designed to make you act immediately without thinking.

Example: "Act now or your account will be permanently deleted in 24 hours."

Why it works: Panic and fear bypass rational thinking.

Protect yourself: Slow down. Legitimate organizations give you time. Verify independently.

Read more on FBI.gov ↗
Module 3

Suspicious Links

Links that redirect you to fake websites designed to look like real ones.

Example: "amazon-secure-login.net" instead of amazon.com

Why it works: URLs can look nearly identical to legitimate domains.

Protect yourself: Always hover over links before clicking. Look for HTTPS and exact domain spelling.

Read more on CISA.gov ↗
Module 4

Impersonation Scams

Scammers pretend to be someone you trust — a boss, bank, or government agency.

Example: "Hi, it's your CEO — please buy $500 in gift cards and send me the codes."

Why it works: Authority and familiarity lower your guard.

Protect yourself: Always verify unusual requests through a separate, trusted channel.

Read more on FTC.gov ↗
Module 5

Lottery & Prize Scams

You "win" a prize for a contest you never entered — but must pay a fee to claim it.

Example: "Congratulations! You've won $10,000. Pay $200 processing fee to claim."

Why it works: Excitement and greed can override skepticism.

Protect yourself: Legitimate prizes never require upfront payment. If you didn't enter, you didn't win.

Read more on FTC.gov ↗
Module 6

Romance Scams

Fake online relationships built over time to gain trust, then exploit you for money.

Example: "I need $300 for a plane ticket to come see you — there was an emergency."

Why it works: Deep emotional investment makes it hard to stay skeptical.

Protect yourself: Never send money to someone you haven't met in person. Use reverse image search on their photos.

Read more on FBI.gov ↗
